Here's to the Crazy Ones, RIP Steve Jobs

Because the people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do.

I was in the hostel mess early in the morning at around 7:30am when I checked my tweets.

It was all over Twitter. And then it was all over Facebook.

How could someone’s death, someone I had never met have such a profound impact upon me? I couldn’t get myself to have food. I kept going through every online article or video about him. Furthermore, I watched Walt Mossberg’s interview at All Things D with Jobs and Gates again. I posted and reposted all over my Twitter & Facebook feeds.

I wanted everyone I knew to know about him. He was a visionary who drove people under him to excellence. They say A good leader takes people where they want to go. But a great leader takes people where they may not want to go but where they should definitely be.

I don’t wish to brush away his dictatorial and rude behavior towards people under the rug. There are umpteen incidents where people had to face his bad side. But I wish to appreciate his contributions and journey as an inspirational story.

He was a person who was adopted, didn’t get a college degree, got kicked out from his own company and spent the best years of his life battling cancer. He did it with a smile and didn’t ask for sympathy.

His death is a loss to the world!

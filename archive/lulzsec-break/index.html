LulzSec Hackers Break Into Their Biggest Target Yet – CIA!

The Hackers group who call themselves Lulz Security have claimed to bringing down the public page of the Central Intelligence Agency – cia.gov

The attack took place on the same day as the group opened a telephone line where fans could suggest potential targets.

Their twitter post was - “Tango down - cia.gov - for the lulz”.

Cia.gov was inaccessible on wednesday but was back up on thursday. It is not known whether the breakdown was due to the attack or the no. of people trying to access the site.

CIA has not confirmed if it was the victim of a DOS (Denial of Service) attack. In a statement a CIA spokesman said, “The CIA’s public web site experienced technical issues that caused it to respond slowly for a short time yesterday evening. Those issues are now resolved.”

The LulzSec have gained nototriety in recent months for breaking down websites of Sony, Nintendo, the US Senate and several other public websites. They call themselves hacktivists, attacking websites of companies who they think have acted against the interests of citizens or consumers.

The Agony and Ecstasy of a Footballer in a Cricket Crazy Nation

Before I start I must tell you that the title was inspired from a post by Raam Shanker - The Agony and Ecstasy of a Football Crazy Nation! And I must say it was a good read.

Coming back, we all are aware of the amount of respect a cricket player gets in this country. And I’m not speaking only about our National team players. This respect & fame is showered even on our Champions League and IPL players. In fact in a building complex if there’s a good cricket player people consider him a local hero.

So it was with a lot of amazement that I heard this story from my friend. He’s a football player (yeah you cricketers can stop reading now and get back to whatever it was you were doing before). No he doesn’t play for any country or club, but he plays for our college, and he’s a good friend. Now KP here was going with the college team to MIT Pune to compete in an inter-college football tournament.

The evening before the team left for Pune, KP was returning from the Sports supply room of our college with his jersey - designed after the Real Madrid team with the number 8 printed on it.

While passing the girls hostel he did what I suspect every guy in the boys hostel does - puffed his chest, straightened his shoulders and tried to look taller than he really is.

When he passed the main entrance 5 or 6 girls started shouting Kaka! Kaka (my mouth is wide open here). Before you get any wrong impression, I’m not saying that girls don’t watch football. I just know the girls of my college to be amazingly not interested in any topic that doesn’t include boy-going-out-with-girl scenario. So my friend is amazed and so am I in its retelling. 5 girls screaming Kaka! How? Do they know the jersey is of Real Madrid? Do they recognize 8 to be Kaka’s jersey number?

I was insanely stupid. For 3 years I had lived with the illusion that all these girls are just not interested in real world matters (things important to ME). And here was proof that I was wrong. I got very excited and asked my friend how did they come to know? They must have definitely recognized the number, no?

He said wait don’t get ahead, the same happened to me. I quote here “I turned around to look at who were the girls, calling my name, & that’s when the world returned to normal. I found out that they were only calling the watchman. Kaka!”

Growing Up in the Middle East...

Hey people, I grew up in the Middle East and the other day I was scrolling on Facebook and a friend linked me this post. It was true and brought a smile to my face. I couldn’t find the original post but have tried to capture the gist of the post.

PLAGIARISM NOT INTENDED

  1. You can’t answer the question, “Where are you from?” (And when you do, you get into an elaborate conversation that gets everyone confused and/or makes you sound very spoiled.)
  2. You flew before you could walk.
  3. You have a passport, but no driver’s license.
  4. You run into someone you know at every airport.
  5. Conversations with friends take place at 6:00 in the morning or 10:00 at night.
  6. Your life story uses the phrase “Then we went to…” five times.
  7. You can speak with authority about the quality of various international airlines.
  8. You feel self-conscious around all white people.
  9. You get offended when someone turns down an offer for food.
  10. You live at school and go home for vacation.
  11. You treasure pork and root beer as highly-valued commodities.
  12. You know the true meaning of “football.” (and in your mind can hear the shout, “GOAL!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!”)
  13. You know that it truly is a small world.
  14. Your wardrobe can only handle two seasons: hot and warm.
  15. You are used to being stared at.
  16. You think VISA is a document stamped in your passport, and not a plastic card you carry in your wallet.
  17. You call a chicken burrito a “shawarma.”
  18. Your dorm room/apartment/living room looks a little like a museum with all the “exotic” things you have around.
  19. Your best friends are from 5 different countries.
  20. You’re spoiled. You know it. You’re VERY spoiled.
  21. You are used to giving directions according to landmarks, not street names.
  22. You didn’t know how to do your own laundry until you left for college.

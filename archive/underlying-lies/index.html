Underlying Lies

We were two travelers in the river of life, it was the same destination but different boats.

We were both the same, but then we were different, Was he like me, or I like him

He saved me in pain when I was low he came, but I left him when I was gay enjoying the inherent life forgetting that I had a saviour like him.

We were together but were miles apart, he had seen pain and suffering and had shielded me from it.

But it was I, not us, him or me; one at a time And we were away from home to face life.

But he asked me to be wary of people they cheat and make you bleed. I took no heed and in the end I did bleed

He ever the friend sat and thoughts I shared, and he showed there was someone who cared!

It was him not me, or was it I not us. If the world were to know we were one they would think us mad, but I cared not!

He was the mask that took over, he was the one who faced the world I had courage not to! We are the underlying lie, he was the face inside my head, screaming to tackle the world all the time, but I had him in chains!

But there are times when I am not in control, when emotions flood me, he breaks free, and wreaks havoc on the world!

I love her, but he hates her, She takes me to a new level, he drags be back, he feels I give too much love to her, if only I could share the same with him.

But I know not why, I am unable to find him in happiness, but now that I come to think I cannot find him these days, he stays away, considers me foolish, maybe.

If only he could know what pain I have now, she has left me, I want to cry, but am I being selfish? Looking for him again in pain?

Here he comes and shouts at me from afar, did I not tell you how women are? Lets us go and spend the rest of our days in peace, reminiscing the days lost,

It is now only him and me, in our old age, walking with help, all glory lost, all glamour gone!

He talks to me and I talk to him, but I still remember her touch, At night I remember her face, the love that we had!

He destroyed me that’s what he did, not letting me love, not letting me play, the mask of a devil was he.

Oh! Here he comes now, I must leave, he may find my things and then make me read, And in these last days if he deserts me, I know not if I shall be able to make the final journey alone!
